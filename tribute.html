Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the 11th President of India and a pioneer of the country's civilian space program and military missile development. He was known as the Missile Man of India for his important role in the nation's civilian space program and military missile development. He also played a pivotal organizational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Achievements & Contributions

Missile Development:

Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to India's missile development efforts, specifically the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP). This initiative saw the development of various missiles:

  • Agni: An intermediate-range ballistic missile, and one of the main achievements of the program.
  • Prithvi: A tactical surface-to-surface short-range ballistic missile.
  • Akash: A medium-range mobile surface-to-air missile defense system.
  • Trishul: A short-range surface-to-air missile.
  • Nag: A third-generation anti-tank missile.

Civilian Space Program:

Dr. Kalam's stint at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is marked with significant achievements. He was the Project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit in July 1980. Under his leadership, ISRO evolved the famous PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) project, which has been a staple for India's space endeavors, launching various satellites into polar and geostationary orbits.

President of India:

Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam's tenure as the President of India from 2002 to 2007 saw numerous instances of his accessibility to the public. He undertook numerous initiatives, focusing particularly on children, often inviting them to the Rashtrapati Bhavan. His presidency was marked by his informal approach and his commitment to inspiring the youth.

Author:

Dr. Kalam was also an acclaimed writer. His book "Wings of Fire" offers deep insights into his life and philosophy. Other notable works include "Ignited Minds", "India 2020", and "My Journey". These books primarily focus on his vision for a developed India and the role of the youth in achieving it.

Educator and Motivational Speaker:

Even after leaving the presidential office, Dr. Kalam took on an educator's role. He visited numerous educational institutions across India, interacting directly with students, addressing their concerns, and motivating them. His lectures, often touching upon India's future development, innovation, and scientific progress, have inspired countless individuals.

Honors and Awards:

Dr. Kalam was the recipient of numerous honors. His contributions to science, technology, and the well-being of India were recognized with awards like the Bharat Ratna (India's highest civilian award), the Padma Vibhushan, and the Padma Bhushan. Internationally, he was nominated for the Youth Icon of the Year award by MTV in 2003, an indication of his significant influence on young people.

Legacy:

Dr. Kalam's unexpected passing on July 27, 2015, was deeply mourned by a nation grateful for his contributions. Despite this loss, his teachings, philosophy, and vision continue to inspire millions, ensuring that his legacy as a scientist, teacher, and leader endures.
